Ancient Sites of Sri Lanka

Anuradhapura

Anuradhapura is an ancient city located in the northern part of Sri Lanka, and it is considered one of the country's most important cultural and historical sites. The city was the capital of Sri Lanka from the 4th century BCE until the 11th century CE, and it was once home to some of the world's most impressive architectural wonders. Today, Anuradhapura is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a popular destination for visitors who want to explore the city's fascinating history and culture. Visitors can explore the city's many ancient temples and palaces, including the Sri Maha Bodhi, a sacred fig tree that is said to be over 2,000 years old.





Polonaruwa

Polonnaruwa is a city in Sri Lanka known for its ancient ruins and historic significance. The city served as the second capital of the country after the fall of Anuradhapura, and was ruled by several Sri Lankan kings during the 11th to 13th centuries. The ruins of Polonnaruwa include the Royal Palace, Council Chamber, Gal Vihara, and various temples and stupas, which provide a glimpse into the country's rich history and cultural heritage. Polonnaruwa is also home to the Parakrama Samudra, a vast man-made reservoir constructed in the 12th century, and serves as an important source of irrigation for the surrounding areas. Today, Polonnaruwa remains a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors from around the world to experience its fascinating history and stunning archaeological sites.
