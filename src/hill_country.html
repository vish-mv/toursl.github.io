Nine Arch Bridge

The Nine Arch Bridge is a popular tourist attraction in Sri Lanka, located in the town of Ella. It is a beautiful, arched bridge that spans a scenic gorge, surrounded by lush greenery and tea plantations. The bridge is made of brick, stone, and cement, and was built during the early 20th century when Sri Lanka was under British colonial rule. The Nine Arch Bridge is a great spot for photography and sightseeing, especially during the early morning or late afternoon when the sun casts long shadows across the bridge. Visitors can access the bridge via a short hike from the nearby train station or road, and there are several viewpoints where you can enjoy the stunning views of the bridge and its surroundings.

Temple Of The Tooth Relic

The Temple of the Tooth Relic, also known as Sri Dalada Maligawa, is a sacred Buddhist temple located in the city of Kandy, Sri Lanka. It is one of the most important religious sites in Sri Lanka and is believed to house the tooth relic of the Buddha. The temple is beautifully designed and decorated, with intricate carvings, golden roof, and colorful murals. Visitors can witness daily rituals and offerings, and also take part in the annual Esala Perahera festival, a grand procession where the sacred tooth relic is paraded around the streets of Kandy. The temple is a must-visit for those interested in Buddhism and Sri Lankan culture, and it is also a UNESCO World Heritage site. Visitors are required to dress modestly and remove footwear before entering the temple complex.

Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage

Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage is a popular tourist attraction located in Sri Lanka. It is a sanctuary for orphaned and injured elephants that have been rescued from the wild. Visitors can observe these majestic creatures up close as they roam, bathe, and interact with one another in their natural habitat. The orphanage also offers opportunities for visitors to bottle-feed baby elephants and witness the daily feeding and bathing rituals. Overall, the Pinnawala Elephant Orphanage provides an unforgettable experience for travelers who want to see elephants in a natural and protected environment.

Lovers Leap Waterfall

Lover's Leap waterfall is a beautiful waterfall located in the central highlands of Sri Lanka. This cascades down from a height of over 30 meters and is surrounded by lush greenery and scenic views. It is a popular tourist attraction for nature lovers and hikers, offering a refreshing break from the city life. Visitors can enjoy a dip in the natural pool at the base of the waterfall or take a refreshing shower under the cascading water. The story of a prince and his lover leaping from a waterfall to their deaths has become a popular legend, leading to the name. The surrounding area also offers plenty of opportunities for trekking, birdwatching, and exploring the local flora and fauna. Overall, this is a must-visit destination for travelers seeking natural beauty and adventure in Sri Lanka.
