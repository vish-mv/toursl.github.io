Sinharaja Forest

Sinharaja is a biologically diverse rainforest situated in the southwestern part of Sri Lanka. This magnificent forest is spread over an area of approximately 18,900 acres and is recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is home to a wide range of flora and fauna, including 19 species of endemic birds, 16 species of endemic reptiles, and a large number of endemic trees, plants, and insects. Sinharaja is also home to several endangered species, such as the Sri Lankan leopard and purple-faced langur. It is an important research site for botanists, zoologists, and other scientists studying the region's natural biodiversity. The forest is also a popular destination for eco-tourists, offering hiking trails, bird watching, and other outdoor activities.

Ella Camping

Ella is a charming hill town situated in the Badulla district of Sri Lanka. It is known for its breathtaking views of lush green hills, tea plantations, and waterfalls. The town's cool climate and picturesque surroundings make it a popular tourist destination. One of the major attractions in Ella is the Ella Rock, a challenging hike that rewards visitors with stunning panoramic views of the surrounding countryside. The town is also famous for its scenic train ride, considered one of the most beautiful train journeys in the world, passing through lush tea estates and misty mountains. Other popular activities in Ella include visiting the Ravana Falls, exploring local temples, and tasting fresh Sri Lankan cuisine.

Meemure Camping

Meemure is a remote village situated in the Knuckles Mountain Range of Sri Lanka. It is a small, isolated community known for its traditional way of life and preserved cultural heritage. The village is surrounded by beautiful mountains, dense forests, and rivers, making it an ideal destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers. Visitors can explore the local customs and traditions, learn about medicinal plants and herbs, and participate in cultural activities such as traditional dances and rituals. Meemure also offers a variety of outdoor activities, including hiking, camping, and white-water rafting. The village can only be accessed by a rough and winding dirt road, adding to its secluded and peaceful atmosphere.

Horton Plains National Park

Horton Plains is a high-altitude plateau located in the central highlands of Sri Lanka. This beautiful natural reserve spans an area of approximately 3,160 hectares and is situated at an elevation of over 2,100 meters above sea level. It is home to a diverse range of flora and fauna, including many endemic species such as the Sri Lankan sambar deer and purple-faced langur. The plains also contain several beautiful waterfalls, including Baker's Falls and the stunning World's End cliff, which offers panoramic views of the surrounding hills and valleys. Horton Plains is a popular destination for hiking and nature lovers, with a variety of trails that offer stunning scenery and unique wildlife viewing opportunities.
